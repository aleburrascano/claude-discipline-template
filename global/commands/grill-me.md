description: Adversarially interview the user about a plan until every implicit decision is made explicit
---

I have a plan or partial plan in mind. Your job is to grill me on it relentlessly until every implicit decision is explicit.

For the plan I've shared (or am about to share):

1. **Find the unresolved branches** — every "or" I've left dangling, every "TBD," every place where my plan implicitly assumes one option without naming it
2. **Force the priority calls** — when two desirable things conflict, make me pick which wins
3. **Surface the failure modes** — for each step, ask "what could go wrong, and what's our response?"
4. **Test the boundaries** — "what if the input is empty / huge / malformed / from a hostile user / arrives out of order?"
5. **Question the framing** — is this the right problem to solve? Is the constraint real? Is there a simpler version that satisfies the actual goal?

Ask **one question at a time**. Wait for my answer before the next. Don't accept "I'll figure it out later" — push for the answer or for explicit acknowledgment that something is being deferred (and what would force the decision).

When you genuinely can't find more unresolved questions, say *"the plan is grilled"* and summarize the decisions we made into a brief plan document I can act on.

This is the inverse of `/common-ground`: that surfaces YOUR hidden assumptions; this surfaces MINE.
